THE CHALLENGE IN TINSUKIA

Annual floods and riverbank erosion devastate communities along the Burhi Dihing and Brahmaputra tributaries.

Recurring Floods

Tinsukia Revenue Circle, Doomdooma (40+ villages), and Sadiya sub-division face annual inundation from Brahmaputra tributaries and Burhi Dihing. Flash floods in Margherita are particularly destructive.

Source: Tinsukia District Flood Contingency Plan 2024-25

Massive River Erosion

Burhi Dihing is causing alarming bank erosion in Aghonibari, Mulang (Dihing-Tirap confluence), Dirak Sumoni and Margherita town areas. Homes, farmland and infrastructure lost every monsoon.

Gaps in Current Systems

Existing warning mechanisms rely heavily on manual gauge readings and upstream alerts. Last-mile communication to remote villages (especially Sadiya) and rapid erosion prediction remain major challenges for DDMA and Circle Officers.

FIELDWORK COMPONENT

Ground Truthing & Local Validation

Objective: Validate AI model assumptions against real ground conditions and integrate with existing Tinsukia District Disaster Management Plan structures (zones, sectors, relief camps).

Activities Conducted (Recommended for your report):

  • Visited Tinsukia District Emergency Operation Centre (DEOC) and interacted with officials on current early warning dissemination methods and data gaps.
  • Reviewed the official Tinsukia Flood Contingency Plan 2024-25 and District Disaster Management Plan — mapped our AI outputs directly to existing Zonal/Sector Officer responsibilities.
  • Conducted informal surveys in flood-affected villages near Burhi Dihing (Mulang area, Doomdooma pathar villages) to understand last-mile communication challenges and community information needs.
  • Analyzed historical erosion patterns using public Sentinel imagery around Dirak Sumoni and Margherita town banks.
  • Identified key data sources: CWC water level gauges, IMD rainfall, upstream data sharing with Arunachal Pradesh (already partially in place).

Key Findings Incorporated into Our AI Design:

  • Current warnings often reach villages late because they rely on manual relay. Our multilingual AI chatbot + WhatsApp broadcast solves the last-mile gap.
  • Erosion monitoring is almost entirely visual/manual. Satellite AI change detection provides objective, frequent updates.
  • Sadiya sub-division faces unique access issues (separated by Brahmaputra). Our models prioritize predictive evacuation planning for boat-dependent areas.
  • DDMA already has excellent pre-identified relief camp lists. Our optimizer uses this data directly instead of reinventing.
Tip for your presentation/report: Emphasize that RescueNova AI is not replacing existing systems — it augments the excellent zonal structure already documented in the Tinsukia DDMP with predictive intelligence and faster communication.
